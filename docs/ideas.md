Piece naming:
- I think "octopus" is a reference to the idea that the knight's grip on all 8 of the squares it can attack is felt, like how an octopus has 8 tentacles. I've only seen this phrase used when a knight is also very strong, typically outposted in enemy territory in a very invasive way, so I think the general meaning of is "strong, and influencing all 8 squares".

- Tall pawn: Humorous construction that alludes to both the larger height of a bishop compared to a pawn, and the worthlessness of such a bishop blocked by its own pawns.

- Pony / Horsie: Very common informal (and sometimes "troll") terms for the Knight.

- Candidate: A Passed Pawn that has a high probability of being promoted to a Queen.

- Wood: A general term for all pieces (e.g., "having more wood" means being up material), but often used to refer to a cluster of pawns.

- Fianchettoed Bishop: A Bishop developed to the long diagonal (g2, b2, g7, or b7) through a hole made by moving a pawn.

- Desperado: A piece that is going to be lost anyway, so it "goes crazy" and captures as much as possible before it is taken.

- Poisoned Pawn: An unprotected pawn that looks like a free capture but leads to a tactical disaster if taken. -> wins the game inmidiatly?

Here are some real ones people actually use.

General piece slang
Woodpusher / Wood
Old-school slang because chess pieces used to literally be wooden.
“Moving the wood” = playing chess.
Men
Classical term. Older books often say “white has two men en prise.”
Still common in commentary.
Material
Collective term for pieces and pawns.
Heavy pieces
Queens and rooks.
Major pieces
Queens and rooks.
Minor pieces
Bishops and knights.
Officers
Older term for non-pawn pieces, especially in European literature.
Army / Forces
Classical descriptive language.
Pawns

Pawns probably have the most specialized vocabulary.

Structural terms
Pawn chain
Connected diagonal pawn structure.
Passed pawn
No enemy pawn can stop it on its file or adjacent files.
Isolated pawn / IQP
Isolated queen pawn. Extremely common terminology.
Doubled pawns
Tripled pawns
Backward pawn
Hanging pawns

These are formal terms, not slang.

Informal / colorful pawn terms
Pawn storm
Aggressive pawn push against castled king.
Pawn roller / Rolling pawns
Connected passed pawns advancing together.
Pawn island
Separate groups of pawns.
Hook
A pawn that gives the opponent a target to open lines against your king.
Very common in modern commentary.
Lever
A pawn break intended to challenge another pawn.
Ram
Two pawns blocked directly against each other. Older Nimzowitsch terminology.
Poisoned pawn
A pawn that can technically be taken but leads to disaster.

Passed pawn = criminal
Famous saying from Aron Nimzowitsch:

“A passed pawn must be restrained. Blockade. Liquidate.”

Also:

“A passed pawn is a criminal which should be kept under lock and key.”

The Harry pawn
Internet slang for the h-pawn.
“Harry the h-pawn marches again.”
The Rookie pawn
Sometimes the a-pawn or h-pawn because beginners neglect them.
Beer pawn / Drunk pawn
Random unnecessary pawn move weakening structure.

Older poetic/classical terminology

bishop as a dragon
